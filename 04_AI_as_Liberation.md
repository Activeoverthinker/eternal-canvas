**For a long time, technology was something I admired from the outside.**

Computers fascinated me, but my lifestyle kept them out of reach. While others grew up online, I grew up in the streets, in punk scenes, in K-holes, in the margins. By the time I finally had stable access to modern tools, I was carrying decades of life that most "tech people" never experience.

**Maybe that's why, when I finally started using AI, I didn't see it as a toy. I saw it as a lever.**

### AI as Tool, Not Master

I have a deep interest in AI development, not in the hardcore coding sense, but in understanding what it's good at, finding its limits, and bending it into shapes that actually help humans instead of just feeding the same sick machine.

**I learned to:**
- Engineer powerful, efficient prompts for my own needs
- Build small workflows
- Create AI agents for different branches of my life where I needed a reliable assistant

**AI has improved many sides of my life:**
- Planning and organizing ideas
- Translating messy memories into clearer text
- Exploring concepts I never had access to in school

**But I'm not naive.**

I know AI can be wrong. I know it's built by humans, with all their biases and blind spots. I know its purpose is to respond to requests based on data and design choices I didn't control.

**So I will never give AI all my trust. I will always stay realistic when using it.**

For me, AI is a powerful screwdriver, not a god.

**This agent:**
